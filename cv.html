Barclays

Design Lead, Sep 2015 – Present

I currently lead a team of designers and researchers to completely re-think how Barclays operates. Specifically looking at how we can become a platform business. The programme was setup to act and behave like a design-thinking start-up / ventures programme.

We work very closely with the product and engineering teams to ideate and build new products and services to connect Barclays' huge network of retail customers (individuals like you and me) and it’s business and corporate customers.

Before that, I was the design lead and team principal for Barclays design language, which is a design system created for native app and responsive web. It allows Barclays to build anything digital at a pace never seen before at the bank. With 3 major brands within the group, we designed and built the system with the concept of the UX is consistant, but the UI is flexible.

Before that, I worked on a programme called CX Transformation which looked at stripping our digital offering back to its skeleton and building a concept bank. Doing the simple things very well and adding innovation. I had the pleasure of presenting it to our CEO as our strategy to compete with new fintech companies looking to capitalise on the Open Banking initiative.

I've also enjoyed working on Barclays Launchpad which is a beta app that allows the design team to quickly get customer feedback on new ideas. One of my ideas was featured on BBC News - Technology﻿

Deltatre

Designer, Nov 2010 – Sep 2015

During my time at Deltatre I was fortunate enough to work with many great clients. Looking back the projects that stood out to me were being the digital partner for Channel 4's debut coverage of the 2012 Paralympic Games, which won our team of 4 a BAFTA for Digital Creativity.

I worked alongside the in-house Olympic team at BBC Sport to design a new video player that integrated live data. That year also marked the BBC's 90th anniversary and they featured it as their latest innovation at the end of this video.

I was part of the team who did the complete redesign of FIFA.com, both visually and structurally. Making it responsive for the first time with live data, a shop and in 6 languages. All in time for the World Cup in 2014. It has stood the test of time as they're still using the same design we did over 5 years ago.
